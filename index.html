The House

A family's home, a city's memory

Mukhi House — also known as Mukhi Mahal — was built in 1920 and served as home to the Mukhi family until Partition compelled them to leave Hyderabad by 1957. In the decades that followed, the building passed between government bodies and endured misuse and civil unrest.

"A palatial home in Sindh with spacious marble courtyards, that appeared to be forever frozen in time."

In 2009, the Sindh Antiquities Department began restoration work, and the Mukhi family gave their blessing for the house to reopen as a public museum. Its carved wooden balconies, ceiling frescoes, and sandstone facade — reminiscent of Italian Renaissance detailing filtered through Sindhi craft — remain intact today.
